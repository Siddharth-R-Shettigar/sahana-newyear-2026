Under the apple tree

In the late 1660s, Isaac Newton watched an apple fall in his family garden and began asking why objects always fall straight toward the Earth. He realised that the same force pulling the apple down also keeps the Moon in orbit and binds planets to the Sun — a universal gravity acting everywhere.

That curiosity led to his laws of motion and gravitation, giving science a new language to describe both everyday motion and the paths of the heavens.

From Newton to Apple

When Apple Computer was founded in 1976, its first logo showed Newton sitting under an apple tree with an apple hanging above his head, celebrating the same moment of insight that revealed gravity. The logo soon evolved into the simple bitten apple, but the spirit of bold, user‑friendly innovation remained and helped Apple grow into one of the most recognised technology companies in the world.

This greeting connects that tradition of discovery with your journey: one thoughtful question today can become tomorrow's breakthrough.
